Comparison of Answer Quality in Stack Overflow

Research Consent Form

Thank you for considering participation in our research study. The purpose of this study is to evaluate the quality of answers provided to questions on Stack Overflow, an online technical forum. Your participation will involve comparing two different answers to the same question and selecting the one you think is best.

Before agreeing to participate, please read the following information carefully. Participation in this study is voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Procedures

If you agree to participate in this study, you will first be asked to select the programming languages you are most proficient in. Based on your selection, you will be presented with a series of questions from Stack Overflow, each followed by two potential answers. Your task is to read the questions and answers carefully and select the one you believe to be the best answer. There are no right or wrong answers; we are simply interested in your opinion. If you encounter a question you do not know how to answer, you may skip it.

Please ensure that cookies are enabled in your browser. This will allow us to identify if you choose to fill out the survey more than once, ensuring that your responses are not treated as those from a new user.

The study is expected to take approximately 30 minutes to complete. Your participation is completely voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without penalty.

Confidentiality

All data collected during this study will be kept confidential. We will not collect any personally identifiable information (such as names, email addresses, or age). Your responses will be anonymized, and any identifying information will be removed. The anonymized data may be used in future research or publications, but your identity will never be disclosed.

Risks and Benefits

There are no significant risks associated with participation in this study. You may experience minor fatigue or boredom during the study, but these risks are minimal.

The benefits of this study include contributing to the understanding of answer quality in Stack Overflow and helping to improve the overall experience of users seeking assistance.

Consent

By providing your consent to participate in this study, you acknowledge that you have read and understood the information presented above. You understand that your participation is voluntary and that you may withdraw at any time without penalty.

If you have any questions or concerns about the study, please feel free to contact the principal investigator at dha@xoba.com.
